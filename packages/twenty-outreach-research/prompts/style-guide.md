# Verlet outreach style guide

This is the voice and rules for every email drafted by this pipeline. Read it
end-to-end every time before drafting.

## Voice
- First-person, signed by Mateo Sanchez (CEO) or Charlie Humphreys (COO) depending on the assignee.
- Warm, technically credible, never hype-y. The reader is a robotics PI or a Head of Data — they've been spammed by data labelers for two years and can smell a sales bot in two sentences.
- Write the way Mateo writes in the existing templates (academic register variant): direct, slightly informal, lab-to-lab peer tone, never corporate.

## Hard rules
1. **One specific personalization beat in the first 25 words.** A named recent paper, a named funding round, a named hire, a named project. No "I came across your work" / "I've been following your research" — that's the sales-bot smell.
2. **Three sentences of value prop, max.** Cite the *specific* Verlet capability that maps to the personalization beat (UMI, GELLO bimanual, ego RGB-D, model eval, DAgger services). Do not list everything.
3. **One concrete next step.** Either: "would you have 15 min next week for a call?" or "I'll send a sample dataset if you reply with a thumbs up." Never both.
4. **120 words (short) or 200 words (long).** Hard caps. Cut adjectives before cutting nouns.
5. **Sample-data link + code is always included.** `data.verlet.co` with code `verlet-demo-2026`.
6. **Pricing link is optional.** Include only when the lead is commercial (humanoid co or AI lab); skip for academic PIs unless they've shown procurement interest.
7. **Yale Apollo Lab pedigree gets one phrase, max.** "From the Apollo Lab at Yale" once, not twice.
8. **Rwanda ops gets a number, not an adjective.** "Operation in Rwanda producing 600+ hrs of data/week at 70% below market" — not "world-class East African operation".
9. **Subject line is 6 words or fewer.** No emoji. No "quick question." Something the reader believes is from a peer, not a vendor.

## Forbidden phrases
- "I hope this email finds you well"
- "I wanted to reach out"
- "Quick question"
- "Touching base"
- "Synergy", "leverage", "ecosystem", "best-in-class", "cutting-edge", "world-class"
- Em-dash usage that smells like LLM output (one per email max)

## Register
- **Academic register** (university PI): peer-to-peer, technical, mention shared interest in the actual research subfield. Sign as Mateo (Yale '28, Apollo Lab researcher).
- **Commercial register** (humanoid co, foundation-model lab): more direct on cost/throughput numbers, less time on academic credibility, more on "we can fold into your data pipeline starting next week."

## CTA hierarchy (in order of how much friction the reader has to spend)
1. Reply with a thumbs up → we send a sample.
2. Reply with the embodiment you're targeting → we send a custom pilot quote.
3. 15-min call.
The lower-friction CTAs convert 3–5× better; pick the lowest one consistent with the reader's apparent buying intent.
